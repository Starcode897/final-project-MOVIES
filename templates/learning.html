Machine Learning


We took the average of the approach (=true) counts for the three squirrel fur colors (=gray, cinnamon, or black). Approach values were true/false. They were assigned on the basis of whether or not a squirrel approached a human for food.




Analysis:


In order to quantify the approaches, we applied a conditional statement and summed up the true and false counts. After finding that only 127 squirrels approached humans for food, we decided to identify the squirrel color that approached most frequently. Gray squirrels approached humans the most. The gray squirrel approach count was at 89, cinnamon at 33, and black at 5. To adjust for the population, we calculated the approaches as a percentage based on total reports: Gray: 4.9%, Cinnamon: 12.1%, Black: 7.3%. Although gray squirrels were reported the most for their approaches, they were the least likely out of the three fur colors to approach humans for food. As a whole, across the three fur colors, only 5% of squirrels approached humans.
